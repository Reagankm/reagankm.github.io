There are a variety of issues that result in a slow draining sewer or sewer back-up. Some of the most common issues are tree roots, oil/grease in the line, or a broken pipe. Trenchless Sewer Replacement

There are a variety of "trenchless sewer replacement technologies" available to repair broken sewer and drain pipe, and some of these techniques can even be used for new installation. Aces Four will assess the condition of your pipe and discuss which method will best serve your family and your property. If you are looking to preserve driveways, patios, walkways, or landscaping, you should definitely check out trenchless sewer replacement. Seattle is well suited to these techniques.

Pipe Bursting

Aces Four was one of the very first sewer contractors in the greater Seattle area to utilize this new technology. Our crews have an unparalleled level of expertise in trenchless pipe replacement. Seattle terrain is perfect for pipe bursting which only requires two excavations. One hole is at the start of the run and one at the end. A concrete bullet/mole is pulled through the old pipe, crushing it, and laying new HDPE pipe behind it. Because pipe bursting allows for the preservation of concrete, asphalt and landscaping, it can be more cost effective than traditional open excavation, since it can eliminate the need for concrete and asphalt removal and replacement. Remember that, depending on the work required, you may need a company like Aces Four Construction, that is registered for work on side sewer in Seattle.

Pipe Lining

Pipe lining can be a great solution when the break is located under a city street, as city backfill and restoration charges can be an extremely costly addition to your repair invoice. The pipe lining method often only requires one excavation (at the start of the run). A high tech polymer liner is installed into the existing pipe. It molds itself to the walls of your pipe, plugging holes and blocking future root intrusion. The perma-liner will cure in a few hours and provides essentially a "new pipe" insert in your existing pipe.

Horizontal Drilling

Also referred to as Directional Boring, this trenchless pipe method can be used for installation of new sewer, drainage, and water line pipe, or for re-routing existing pipes. Only requiring two excavations (one at the start of the run and one at the end), horizontal drilling lays new pipe in a straight line between the two excavations. Directional boring can save time and money, as well as protecting existing concrete and asphalt and preserving landscape.

Water in Your Basement or Crawl Space?

If water is getting into your basement or crawlspace, a problem which can occur when current footing drain or downspout systems fail or when groundwater conditions around your property have changed, this can be quite damaging. It not only affects your carpet/flooring, but also presents dangers of mold, humidity, and structural damage to your home.

Our estimator will meet you at your property to inspect the drainage situation, discuss options, and provide an estimate for how best to protect your house.

Often the best solution is an exterior footing drain system (i.e. repairing an existing system or installing a new foundation drain system). Aces Four can excavate to expose your foundation and install footing drains (perforated pipe surrounded in washed rock) to collect the ground water and move it away from your house. We seal coat the exposed portion of the foundation wall to prevent further water infiltration.

Environmentally Friendly Drainage Solutions

Drainage systems can include green technology such as rain gardens, new advances in bioretention cells which help remove contaminants from water runoff before it enters the storm system.

Rain Gardens

Rain gardens, also called bioretention cells, are planted depressions designed to allow storm water to absorb back into the ground rather than flow into storm drains. They have been shown to significantly reduce the amount of pollution reaching local waterways and because they're so effective at capturing water they can also help to reduce flooding. Because of their many benefits some local governments in the Puget Sound area have incentive programs for installing a rain garden on your property. To see if you qualify, check out the programs listed at the 12,000 Rain Gardens Incentives page.
